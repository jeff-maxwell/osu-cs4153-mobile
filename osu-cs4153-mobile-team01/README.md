# OSU CS4153 Mobile App Development

## Team Assignment 01 – 20 Points

#### Last updated: Sep 5, 2018 2:30 PM 
#### Due: Sep 27 11:59 PM

Your team is to deliver a stylish and sturdy implementation of a Wheel of Fortune type of
game app. This will give you and your teammates a chance to get to know one another’s skills,
to learn to work together effectively before the bigger project, and perhaps have a little fun
along the way.

Wheel of Fortune is a US television game show loosely based on the simpler game Hangman.
Your game should meet the following specifications:

- Name your project:
    T01_teamColor

where teamColor is your team color, only (i.e., Red, Green, Blue, etc.).

- Design and write your app specifically for the iPhone 8 Plus.

- Your team may choose to make the app operate in portrait orientation, landscape
orientation, or both.

- Come up with a unique theme and name for your game.

- While the TV show has a wheel that spins, your team is not required to include a wheel.
Instead, you could choose some other, clever way of performing the same selection
process.

- Your team may choose to make this a solo or multi-player (on the same device) game.

- Make effective use of color, graphics, audio, and gestures.

- Your team is encouraged to create some or all of its own graphics assets, using products
such as the GIMP, Photoshop, etc. You may use graphics from other sources, but not
from other match-up games.

- When the app starts, it should display a splash screen (i.e., an introductory screen) with
a stylish “play” button. When the user taps the “play” button, segue to the actual game-
playing screen. (We have not yet covered segues, but will; you can read about segues in
textbooks you can download from the library and on RayWenderlich.com.)

- Create a PDF document utilizing a word processor. In the document, describe how to
play the game. If your team has utilized any non-Apple classes, assets, etc. that it did
not create, they must be reference in a separate section of the document. If you have
some special features of the app you want to make sure the grader sees, highlight them
in a section of the document. Also include a section that describes what work was done
by each member of the team.

### General Notes
- Do not copy the code of any such game you find online or elsewhere. This is considered
cheating. You may, however, refer to such games to get ideas. Remember: if you are
uncertain as to whether your use of such materials is appropriate, ask the instructor.

- Everyone should have coding tasks to perform, if possible; the team should meet as
needed to discuss app design and details, and to share new things learned that might be
useful to all teammates.

- COMMUNICATION IS CRITICAL! You should make every effort to attend (in person or
by Skype) every team meeting. If you can’t make it to a meeting, contact a teammate to
learn what transpired.

### Grading Notes
- Creativity, style, and correct operation will be among the factors used to grade the
projects.
- The instructor will select the app that he feels best achieves creativity, style, and correct
operation, and each member of that team will receive a 5-point bonus. The decision of
the instructor is final.
